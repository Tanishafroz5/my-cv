Cox’s Bazar is a town on the southeast coast ofIt’s known Bangladesh for its very long, sandy beachfront, stretching from Sea Beach in the north to Kolatoli Beachinthe south. Aggameda Khyang monastery is home to bronze statues and centuries-old Buddhist manuscripts. South of town, the tropical rainforest of Himchari National Park has waterfalls and many birds. North, sea turtles breed on nearby Sonadia Island.

Rangamati (Bengali: )is the administrative headquarters of Rangamati Hill District in the Chittagong Hill Tracts of Bangladesh. The town is located at 22°37'60N 92°12'0E and has an altitude of 14 metres (46 ft).[1] The district is administered by an office named as District Administration, Rangamati.
